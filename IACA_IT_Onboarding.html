Welcome to the IACA team!

Follow the steps below from top to bottom. Each section can be expanded. Once you've finished, complete the final checklist so IT knows you're all set.

Step 1

Get to Know Microsoft 365

IACA uses Microsoft 365 for virtually all day-to-day work. Here are the main tools you'll use:

Microsoft Teams
Internal chats, video calls, and team channels. Your primary communication hub.
Outlook
Email and calendar management. Access via browser or the desktop app.
SharePoint
IACA's intranet and shared document storage. You're here right now.
OneDrive
Your personal cloud file storage. Keep your work files here and access them anywhere.
Power Automate & Apps
Workflow automation and custom tools used by specific teams. IT will guide you if needed.

Access all Microsoft 365 apps at office.com — just log in with your IACA email and password.

Step 2

Access Your Email (Outlook)

You can access your IACA email in two ways:

1
Web browser (recommended for first access)

Go to outlook.office.com, sign in with your IACA email address and temporary password.

2
Desktop App (Outlook)

Open Outlook on your computer. When prompted, add your IACA email account. IT can assist with the initial setup if needed.

3
Mobile (optional)

Install Microsoft Outlook from the App Store or Google Play and sign in with your IACA account to access email on the go.

First login: You will be asked to change your temporary password immediately. See Step 5 for instructions.

Tip: Set up your email signature in Outlook settings. IT can share the standard IACA signature template on request.

Your email format is: firstname.lastname@iaca.int

Step 3

Get Started with Microsoft Teams

1
Download & install Teams

Download Microsoft Teams for your device at teams.microsoft.com/download, or access it in your browser at teams.microsoft.com.

2
Sign in

Use your IACA email address and password. If prompted for MFA, see Step 6.

3
Join your team channels

Your manager or IT will add you to relevant team channels. You can also find teams using the "Teams" tab on the left-hand side panel.

4
Set your status & notifications

Click your profile picture in the top right to set your availability status and configure your notification preferences to avoid overload.

Tip: Use the Teams calendar feature for scheduling meetings — it syncs automatically with your Outlook calendar.

Step 4

SharePoint & Working with Files

SharePoint is IACA's intranet — it hosts team pages, announcements, policies, and shared document libraries.

1
Access SharePoint

Go to iaca1.sharepoint.com and sign in with your IACA account. You can also reach it via office.com → SharePoint.

2
Navigate your team site

Each department has its own SharePoint site. Your manager will share the link to your team's site. Use the top navigation to browse pages.

3
Work with documents
  • Open files directly in the browser (Word, Excel, PowerPoint)
  • Use Sync to access SharePoint folders from Windows Explorer
  • Co-edit documents in real time with colleagues
  • Right-click → Share to share a link to any file

OneDrive is your personal file space — use it for files you're working on individually before sharing.

A
Access OneDrive

Go to onedrive.com or find it in the Microsoft 365 app launcher (the 9-dot grid).

B
Sync to your computer

Install the OneDrive desktop sync client and sign in. Your files will appear directly in File Explorer / Finder for easy offline access.

C
Scanning & uploads

The printer can scan documents directly to your OneDrive — see Step 7 for details.

Store shared/team files on SharePoint, not OneDrive. OneDrive is for personal or draft documents only.

Step 5

Change Your Password

1
First-time login

When you first sign into any Microsoft 365 service, you will be automatically prompted to create a new personal password. Do this before anything else.

2
Change password later

Go to myaccount.microsoft.com → Password → Change password. Enter your current password, then your new one.

3
Forgot your password?

Use the Self-Service Password Reset at aka.ms/sspr. You'll need to have set up MFA first (see Step 6). Alternatively, contact IT directly.

Password rules: Minimum 8 characters, must include uppercase, lowercase, a number, and a symbol. Do not reuse recent passwords.

Step 6

Set Up Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds a second layer of security to your account — even if someone gets your password, they can't log in without your phone. This is mandatory at IACA.

1
Go to security setup

Visit aka.ms/mfasetup and sign in with your IACA account.

2
Add a sign-in method

Click "+ Add method" and choose one of the options below (Microsoft Authenticator is recommended).

3
Follow the setup wizard

For the Authenticator app: download the app on your phone → scan the QR code shown on screen → enter the verification code to confirm.

4
Test it

Sign out and sign back in — you should now be prompted for a second verification. Contact IT if you encounter issues.

Available methods

Microsoft Authenticator App ⭐ Recommended
Approve sign-ins with a tap — fast and secure.
SMS Text Message
Receive a code via text message to your phone number.
Email (backup)
Use a personal email as a backup recovery method.
Step 7

Using the Printer (Konica Minolta Workplace Pure)

IACA uses Konica Minolta's Workplace Pure system for printing and scanning.

✅ Print from / scan to OneDrive
✅ Scan to email
✅ Print multiple PDFs at once
✅ Badge or PIN login

Getting Started

1
Driver installation (IT will do this)

IT will install the Konica Minolta Workplace Pure printer driver on your computer. It will then appear in your list of printers.

2
First-time login

When you print for the first time, a login window appears on the right side of your screen. Select the IACA domain, then sign in with your IACA email and password.

3
Badge & PIN setup

IT will link your staff badge to the printer. If you forget your badge, you can also print using a PIN code (assigned by IT).

How to Print

A
From your computer
  • Select Konica Minolta Workplace Pure from your print dialog
  • To print multiple PDFs: drag and drop them into the print window
  • Walk to the printer, then tap your badge or search your name and enter your PIN
  • Select which jobs to print, then confirm
B
Print from OneDrive (Pull Print)

Use the Pull Print feature on the printer screen to access your OneDrive directly and print files from there. PDFs work best.

How to Scan

C
Scan to OneDrive or Email
  • On the printer, tap Scan
  • Choose OneDrive (recommended) or Email
  • Optionally: rename the file, choose format (PDF, JPG…), adjust settings, pick a OneDrive folder

Prefer scanning to OneDrive over email to avoid filling up your mailbox.
